A mug is a type of cup typically used for drinking hot drinks, such as coffee, hot chocolate, or tea. Mugs usually have handles[1] and hold a larger amount of fluid than other types of cup

Things to consider before buying a coffee mug

Material
    Coffee mugs are made from a variety of materials. There is no best material to use, only what suits your tastes.
     However, the most practical coffee mug is an insulated stainless steel mug. These mugs are durable and will keep your coffee warmer for longer. 
     They often have a lid and are commonly used as a travel mug for long journeys. Stainless steel is notoriously resilient and
      will survive any amount of heavy-handedness. Still, try not to drop it all the time. 
     Ceramic mugs are environmentally friendly and retain a certain amount of heat. A ceramic mug doesn’t retain old flavors or smells.
    If you have a sleek-looking home, you will want a coffee mug to match. Glass coffee mugs look unique and classy.
    Fine china cups are made from porcelain and are lightweight and delicate. These items are usually used to impress guests at parties.
    However, they are too fragile for everyday use.
                
     Weight 
    If you or your visitors struggle with lifting heavy mugs, you will want to choose a lightweight mug. You can look specifically for 
    lightweight coffee mugs. If possible, go to a store and take the coffee cup in your hand. Judge the weight yourself. Lightweight mugs are great for older people and those with arthritis or fibromyalgia.
                
    Handles
    If you or a loved one struggle to hold a single-handled coffee mug, you can buy a mug with two handles.
     Depending on the shape of your hand, you may have a particular handle shape in mind. For example, a “C” handle is the most popular 
     style of handle for coffee mugs. It has a rounded shape and is easy to grip. A “D” handle is more of a rectangular shape than a “C” handle.
      It offers a little more space for those with larger fingers and hands.
     Open-handled mugs might be easier to hold than the others. If you suffer from
      arthritis you can grip them without
      needing to squeeze your fingers through a small gap. Open-handled mugs are also easier to hang on a mug tree.
                
                In addition, a curved handle will make a plain-looking mug look more elegant.
